by Patrisia Gonzales

Section: I Mind

Quote: The trauma is held personally and transmitted over generations

Generational trauma is apparent in various different areas of a person's life. Because of this while reading the article I stop and look at my own life to see if I can pinpoint any times that I myself have been affected by generational trauma. I believe that through doing this I am learning more about myself as well as the people that came before me.

Section: II Body

Quote: What happen to love?

While reading through the words of Patrisia Gonzales I do not seem to feel any difference regarding my physical state. I see this as a good thing as this means I am in a state of comfort and balance. Gonzales places a large emphasis on love and whether the trauma caused by history has made it disappear. This causes some tension as I stop to analyze the validity of this statement. Personally I feel this to not be the case as I feel what people describe as "love" to be a human instinct. Without love there would be no reason to go on. This article, class and the fact that I am writing this today is a testament that love has not gone away.

Section: III Emotion

Quote: Why is it some of us but not all assume the burden strap and carry the grief for our people's suffering?

The feelings that present themselves while looking into what Patrisia Gonzales has written is frustration and anger. The reason for this, is that the reality that generational trauma exists is a sad one. Regardless of the ways in which it can be combated it is still a pitiful reality that many people have to face every day. Because of this I feel the emotions that I feel to be warranted.

Section: IV Spirit

Quote: Trauma is a spirit that must be left offerings

I would consider myself to be receptive to sparks of creativity and inspiration. One example of this would be the inspiration to look at practical tips that can be given to combat generational trauma. While I agree that large systemic changes are helpful I also agree that individual responsibility plays a major role. A great analogy is that a forest is made up of many trees. A large change is made up of multiple small ones.
